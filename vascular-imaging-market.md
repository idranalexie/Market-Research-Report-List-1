<p><p>The vascular imaging market comprises various modalities used to visualize blood vessels and identify vascular diseases. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) provides detailed images of soft tissues and blood flow. Computed Tomography (CT) offers rapid and accurate cross-sectional views of blood vessels. Ultrasound uses sound waves to capture real-time images and assess circulation. Nuclear imaging employs radioactive tracers to evaluate vascular function. X-ray technology provides quick assessments of vascular structures, often utilizing contrast agents for enhanced visualization. Each modality has unique strengths suited for different diagnostic needs.</p></p>
