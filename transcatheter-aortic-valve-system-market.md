<p><p>The Transcatheter Aortic Valve System (TAVR) is a minimally invasive procedure utilized to treat aortic stenosis, a condition where the heart's aortic valve narrows, hindering blood flow. TAVR involves the placement of a new valve via catheterization, usually through the femoral artery, which reduces recovery times and hospital stay compared to traditional surgical methods. </p><p>The market for TAVR is experiencing significant growth, driven by an increasing prevalence of aortic stenosis, advancements in valve technology, and a growing geriatric population. Furthermore, rising healthcare expenditure and improvements in healthcare infrastructure contribute to this expansion.
